What to eat?

Nothing beats Thailand when it comes to food! From unprepossessing food stalls, fancy restaurants to buzzing night markets,I've tried them all! Here are some MUST-TRY food I had during my trip that you definitely do not want to miss out!

1. No.9 2nd Restaurant

One of the best restaurants I tried in Phuket by far, allow me to introduce - No.9-2nd Restaurant! Not to be confused with No.9 Restaurant or the famous No.6 Restaurant (which are all located in Patong), No.9 2nd Restaurant serves up some of the best local thai cuisine that is affordable and delicious. Besides the traditional thailand cuisines, they also serve western and japanese cuisine, so if you are looking for something different, they got you covered! While the shop was a little small, it has a nice ambience and gives off a cozy vibe.

Interior of No.9 2nd Restaurant, looks cozy and homey!

I ordered their famous Pad-thai, prawn-egg omelette, and the lime-basil steam fish, which comes in generous portion. I especially LOVED the steam fish, which was not only fresh, but also juicy and tangy at the same time! The staffs here were also super polite and professional, which was a huge plus point. them were top notch. Overall, all the dishes I tried are absolutely worth it and I would definitly visit again when I come back to Phuket. Next time if you are looking for some authentic thailand cuisine in Phuket, don't forget to give No.9 2nd restaurant a try!

Lime-Basil steam fish that I ordered, yummy!

2. Malin Plaza Nightmarket (Basil pork rice)

During one of my nightmarket food-hunt in phuket, I chanced upon this unassuming stir-fry store located in the Malin Plaza. I decided to give it a shot and it turns out it was one of the most memorable meals I had in my entire trip! I ordered their signature basil pork fried rice, which comes with 2 large fried eggs. Juicy and flavourful minced pork stir-fry with red-hot chili and basil, this dish is truly to die-for!

Pork Basil Rice, definitly worth the 100 baht!

3. Malin Plaza Nightmarket (Moo-Ping)

Besides stir-fry dishes, they also sell grill meat skewers which are grilled-upon-order to ensure its served to you piping hot! I ordered some of their popular Moo Ping and the flavors were absolutely incredible, with a perfect blend of savory and slightly sweet spices. Choose between different types of meat - chicken, pork, beef or have them all!

Meaty and delicious Moo Ping (3 for 100 baht!)

Tips and Tricks You Need To Know!

Want to explore Phuket like a true local? We got you covered! Based on my own experiences, I have compiled a list of useful and practical tips that will make your trip easier and better!

1. Download the Bolt app

Have you ever notice that almost no locals uses TukTuk or Taxis? That's because they don't! And for good reasons. While most locals would have their own modes of transport, there are still a handful that do not. If you are travelling to Phuket, you will realise the price of TukTuk, Taxis and even Grab are pretty pricey.

During my trip, I discovered that the locals there actually uses a ride-hailing app called bolt. Here in Phuket, Bolt is their go-to. The prices of each ride are way cheaper and its also easier to find a driver. The app is available in app store and also comes in english. It is simple and easy to use, so new-users like yourself don't have to worry about it.

Don't forget to download the app before going to Phuket!

2. Bring your own essentials before going for the Island Hopping Tour.

If you ever decide to book yourself an Island Hopping Tour, then you will find these tips extremely useful.

First, bring your own waterproof phone holder if you want to take photos and videos when you are snorkling during the tour. Even if you do not have one, buy it in nightmarkets beforehand! While the staffs at the tour do sell these phoneholders, it comes at nearly twice the price.

Second, bring lots of sunscreen, you'll need it! The heat in Phuket is unforgiving, and if you are going on a full-day tour out in the middle of the ocean, you'll definitly need lots of sunscreen to prevent sun burn. Ouch! You can easily buy sunscreen at any local convinient stores or pharmacys and I promise you its worth it!

Third, bring a set of spare clothes. After one whole day of island hopping, snorkling and swimming, you'll want to change to a fresh set of clothes. Plus, if you are planning to head out to other attractions after the tour, you can do so without feeling sweaty and wet.

3. Get a DTAC tourist simcard

If you purchase overseas 4G from your local telco provider, it usually cost $5 for 1GB. But with DTAC Happy tourist sim card, you can get up to 30GB of data for 16 days at less than $10! You can purchase the simcard via Klook and pick up Phuket's airport on the day of your arrival. Easy and fuss-free! They also have options to pickup from BangKok's airport for those who are coming from Bangkok.

Simply present your voucher upon buying from the Klook to redeem your sim card!
