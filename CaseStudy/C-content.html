Case Study: Travel Blog Website Design

Project Overview: Get Lost - And Find Yourself.

Travel blog: Get Lost - And Find Yourself.

About

“Get Lost – And Find Yourself” is a travel blog where I showcase and share my travelling experiences while providing inspiration and tips for other like-minded travelers. In this blog, readers are provided with a comprehensive travel guide which includes information such as food recommedations, attractions to visit, accomodations to stay, as well as some practical tips and tricks.

Objectives

To design and develop a travel blog microsite that is both engaging, functional and promotes travelling. The travel blog should be aesthetic and appealing, where users can easily navigate and browse the site with ease.

My Role

Design thinking process from conceptualization to implementation. This includes:

  • User Research (Interviews)

  • User Research Findings Analysis (Customer Journey Map & User Persona)

  • Define Problem Statement

  • Ideation

  • Prototype Creation

  • Usability Testing

  • Post-UT Analysis

Phase 1 : Understand

  • Empathize
  • Define
Empathize

The first stage of the design thinking process is to empathize. By empathizing with the users, I will be able to better understand their needs, motivations and identify their pain points when it comes to browsing and navigating a travel blog. To achieve this, I conducted user experience research by recruiting 5 participants in a user interview.

User Experience Research

Research scope

  • To find out: What motivates users to read travel blogs? What content do users find interesting and engaging?

  • “Must-knows”: What specific features or functions do users expect on a travel blog? What information or content do users expect to find on the travel blog?

  • “Good-to-know”: What is the user’s attitude towards traveling and what are their preferred travel styles?

Research Type: Qualitative

Research Method: User Interviews

Number of Participants: 5

Interview Questions:

    1. What motivates you to read travel blogs?

    2. What kind of travel-related content are you most interested in?

    3. What information do you usually look for when planning to travel?

    4. Are there any specific features or functions you look for in a travel blog?

    5. How important is the visual design and overall aesthetic of a travel blog to you?

Define

The second stage of the design thinking process is to define. Based on the insights gathered from the empathy stage, I can now start to define the problem and identify opportunities for innovation. To do this, I created a user persona and a customer journey map to better understand the user's goals, pain points, and emotions.

Interview Findings

Below is a summary of the findings derived from the participant’s responses.

  • Participants choose to read travel blogs as it gives them a more personal touch, offering more authentic travelling experiences rather than popular tourist destinations.

  • Participants are interested in short-form content that provides practical tips and guides, accompanied with visual content such as photos and videos.

  • Participants look for information relating to food recommendations, accommodations, unique attractions, and useful tips & tricks.

  • Participants look out for features like hyperlinks that can take them to relevant sites such as booking sites and food locations. Some also mentioned that a user-friendly and easy to navigate interface would be useful in finding information easily.

  • Many participants feel that the aesthetic and visual design of a travel blog is important in capturing their attention.

From the interview findings, I created a customer journey map and customer persona to illustrate the needs of my user when potentially interacting with my blogsite.

Customer Journey Map

User Persona

With these findings, I now have a better idea of the exact problems my users could potentially face. As such, I have created a problem statement.

Problem Statement

Travel blog readers wants to read visually appealing travel blogs with personal and authentic travel experiences which contains information such as food recommendations, accommodations, attractions, and tips because many existing travel blogs are too wordy and boring. Additionally, readers also lookout for a user-friendly interface, engaging contents and useful features such as hyperlinks on the blog site.

Phase 3 : Materialize

  • Test

Usability Testing

After designing the prototypes, I then proceed to conduct a usability testing (UT) with users. Conducting a UT will therefore help to gather relevant insights and feedback from users, identifying any improvements before implementation.

Goal: To evaluate user interface and user experience of the new travel blog microsite, identify areas of improvement and if it meets the needs of travel blog readers.

Scope: Process from navigating website homepage to finding relevant travel information on the website. Remote Qualitative Usability Test (UT), moderated.

Profile: Travel enthusiast, individuals who frequently reads travel blogs.

No. of Participants: 5

    Examples of usability tasks in discussion guide.

    Post-UT Analysis

    By observing how the users interact and perform their tasks, I was able to identify areas of improvements on the current layout and design. Below are some of the key findings derived from the UT test.

    Key Findings

      1. Homepage & Travel Blog page: User-friendly navigation, good amount of photos and videos.

      2. Homepage: Visually appealing, good design layout.

      3. Travel Blog page: Good use of hyperlinks.

      4. Travel Blog page: Overall aesthetic is lacking; inconsistent image sizes.

      5. Travel Blog page: Layout looks messy and cluttered, flow looks unnatural.

    Refining the design

    From the test findings, I then proceed to rework on the website design layout and created a low-fidelity prototype that incoperated the key findings above. Subsequently, I designed the microsite based on the new prototype before implmenting it in the next stage.
